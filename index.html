Upper Body Foundation

The push-up is not basic.
It's fundamental.

The push-up is the single most efficient bodyweight exercise ever created. It trains your chest, shoulders, triceps, and core -- all at once, anywhere, with zero equipment.

Functional strength Builds real-world pushing power that translates to daily life and sport.
Core stability Every rep forces your core to brace and stabilize, building an iron midsection.
Joint health Strengthens the shoulder girdle and wrists, preventing injuries long-term.
Core Command

A strong core is a
strong life.

Your core is the bridge between upper and lower body. Without it, every movement leaks power. Sit-ups train the rectus abdominis and hip flexors, building a stable, resilient trunk.

Better posture A strong core holds your spine in alignment, reducing back pain and slouching.
Athletic performance Power in every sport originates from the core -- running, throwing, lifting, jumping.
Muscular endurance High-rep sit-ups build the stamina your midsection needs for prolonged effort.
Fuel The Machine

You can't out-train
a bad diet.

Nutrition isn't about perfection -- it's about consistency in your choices. Whole foods, adequate protein, proper hydration, and eating with intention will get you further than any supplement ever will.

Protein first Aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg of bodyweight. Prioritize whole sources: eggs, fish, poultry, legumes.
Hydration matters Dehydration drops performance by up to 25%. Drink before you feel thirsty.
Meal consistency Eat at regular intervals. Your body thrives on rhythm -- feed it predictably.
