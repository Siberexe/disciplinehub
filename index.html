Foundation Movement

The Power of
Push-Ups

Push-ups are the cornerstone of bodyweight training. They build raw upper body strength, improve posture, and forge mental toughness — one rep at a time.

Push-ups engage your chest, shoulders, triceps, and core simultaneously. They're scalable — from wall push-ups for beginners to explosive clap variations for advanced athletes. Doing push-ups consistently improves bone density, increases metabolic rate, and builds functional strength that translates to everyday movements. Start with 3 sets of 10 and progressively increase volume weekly.

Core Strength

Sit-Ups:
Build The Core

A strong core is the foundation of every athletic movement. Sit-ups target your abdominals, hip flexors, and lower back — building a solid center of power.

Beyond aesthetics, sit-ups improve spinal stability, reduce back pain, and enhance balance. Combine standard sit-ups with crunches, leg raises, and planks for complete core development. Aim for 4 sets of 15-20 reps with controlled movement — never sacrifice form for speed. Your core is your armor. Build it strong.

Fuel Your Body

Nutrition
Fundamentals

Training breaks muscle down. Nutrition builds it back stronger. What you eat determines whether your effort in the gym pays off or gets wasted.

Focus on whole foods: lean proteins for recovery, complex carbohydrates for energy, and healthy fats for hormonal balance. Eat to fuel performance, not to fill emotions. Hydration is equally critical — aim for at least 3 liters of water daily. Your diet is your secret weapon.
